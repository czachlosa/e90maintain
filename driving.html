DRIVING MANUAL

Approach

Be mindful of your surroundings as you approach the car, keeping an eye out for obstacles, pedestrians, and other cars. Be sure to approach from a safe side, preferably the side of the car that is closest to the curb or away from oncoming traffic. Make sure the car is safe to get into and that nothing is blocking the tires or doors by visually inspecting it.

Enter

Use the key, fob, or handle sensor to unlock the door, then cautiously open it without swinging it into oncoming traffic or objects. To make sure the door is securely latched, sit in the driver's seat and close it. Make sure all personal belongings are safe and do not obstruct the steering wheel or pedals while you take a moment to relax.

Adjust

With a small bend in your knees, you can comfortably reach the pedals by moving the seat forward or backward. If necessary, raise or lower the seat to get a good view of the dashboard and the road ahead. Adjust the side and rearview mirrors to reduce blind spots, and place the steering wheel so that your hands can comfortably rest on it while you still have complete control.
